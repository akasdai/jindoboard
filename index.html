The Korean Jindo — Korea's National Treasure

Full Breed Guide →

Designated as Natural Monument No. 53, the Korean Jindo (진돗개) is celebrated for unmatched loyalty, sharp intelligence, and a proudly independent spirit. Originating from Jindo Island in southwestern Korea, this ancient breed has earned admirers around the globe and stands as a living symbol of Korean heritage.

🏝️

Jindo Island Origin

Named after Jindo Island (진도) off the southwestern coast of South Korea, the breed developed naturally over centuries, shaped by the island's rugged terrain and the practical needs of Korean farmers and hunters.

🤝

Legendary Loyalty

Jindos form deep, lifelong bonds with their primary owner. Stories of Jindos traveling hundreds of kilometers to return home have become part of Korean cultural folklore — a testament to their unwavering devotion.

🏆

Internationally Recognized

One of only two Korean breeds recognized by the Fédération Cynologique Internationale (FCI), the Jindo is also designated a Korean National Monument — a source of immense national pride and cultural identity.

🎨

Six Beautiful Colors

Jindos come in six recognized coat colors: white (백구), fawn (황구), brindle (호구), black & tan (흑구), grey (회구), and wolf grey (재구). Each variation is uniquely stunning and equally magnificent.
